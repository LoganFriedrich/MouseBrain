POTENTIAL MENINGEAL ARTIFACT:
------------------------------------------------------------
  The R > L bias is concentrated in surface-adjacent brainstem
  regions (ventral and lateral surfaces), while deep/midline
  structures are near-symmetric. This spatial pattern is consistent
  with residual meningeal tissue on the right brain surface causing
  false-positive cell detections that get atlas-assigned to the
  nearest legitimate brain region.

  WHAT THE CURRENT FILTER CATCHES:
    - Entire region types that shouldn't contain spinal-projecting
      neurons (cerebellar cortex, white matter, olfactory, cortical
      layers 1-3, hippocampus, basal ganglia)

  WHAT IT CANNOT CATCH:
    - Meningeal false positives that are spatially near legitimate
      brainstem regions. These get atlas-registered to the nearest
      brain region (e.g., GRN, IRN, MARN) and pass all filters.
    - The filter works by region IDENTITY, not spatial LOCATION
      (surface vs. deep within a region).
